# Inkline

Drop your Apple Health export in a browser tab and get back a storybook of your running year — chapters, beasts, and verse, all pulled out of the same GPS points your watch was quietly recording the whole time.

## Try it

**[rohnach29.github.io/inkline](https://rohnach29.github.io/inkline/)**

There's a demo book on the landing page if you want to see what comes out the other end before you hand over your own data.

## What it makes

Inkline turns a year of runs into a small book. It finds your first run, your last one, the fastest, the longest, the ones that happened at 2am for no defensible reason — and gives each its own chapter, with a title, a kicker, a few stats, and up to six lines of verse.

It also collects **beasts**: recurring patterns worth naming, like a hill you keep losing to, or a stretch of forty quiet days. Here's one chapter's verse, in full:

> But quiet isn't empty — ask the snow.
> It's just the road rehearsing where you'll go.

And a line from the chapter about the time your data crossed an ocean:

> You went so far the map ran out of ink,

The verse isn't generated by anything probabilistic — it's assembled from a hand-written couplet library, with rhyme and scansion enforced by tests. Same input, same couplets, every time.

## Privacy

Everything happens in the tab. There's no server, no accounts, no analytics — zero network requests after the page loads, which you can confirm yourself by opening the network panel and watching nothing happen. Your export never leaves your machine.

## Getting your Apple Health export

1. Open the **Health** app on your iPhone and tap your profile picture, top right.
2. Tap **Export All Health Data** and wait for it to zip.
3. Share the zip to your computer (AirDrop, email, cable, whatever gets it there) and drop it on the Inkline page.

Don't have an iPhone, or just want to try a single route? Inkline also accepts loose GPX files — drop one in and it'll build what it can from that alone.

## How it works

```
ingest      unzips the export (or reads the GPX) and normalizes every run
analyze     finds the extremes, the streaks, the beasts worth naming
storytell   picks couplets and stats for each chapter, deterministically
render      lays it out and draws the book you actually see
```

## Determinism

The same export produces the same book, every time — same chapters, same verse, same order. The pipeline is seeded and tested against a golden snapshot, so there's no hidden randomness between one run of the page and the next.

## Tech notes

- No UI framework — it's built directly on the DOM.
- Two runtime dependencies: `fflate` to unzip the export, `tz-lookup` to resolve timezones.
- Every timestamp is converted to local time using the GPS coordinates of that specific run, not your device's clock or the export's filename — both lie the moment you get on a plane.
